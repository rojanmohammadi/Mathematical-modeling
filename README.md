# Mathematical Modeling in Cancer & Evolutionary Biology 🧬

This repository features computational simulations and theoretical frameworks developed for the Mathematical Modeling in Cancer module. The project explores the intersection of stochastic processes, population genetics, and the emergence of complexity in tumor evolution.

## 🎯 Key Learning Objectives & Applied Skills

### 🎲 Stochastic Programming & Probabilistic Modeling
- **Implementation:** Drawing from probability distributions to simulate 1-dimensional random walks and stochastic trajectories.
- **Statistical Analysis:** Calculating mean and variances from large-scale random data sets to analyze population-level averages.

### 🧬 Cancer Evolution Frameworks
- **Multi-stage Carcinogenesis:** Programming the **Armitage and Doll model** to investigate stochastic mutations, single/multiple hit hits, and waiting times to cancer.
- **The Moran Process:** Implementing the stochastic Moran process to study fixation probabilities, fixation times, and the role of selection in finite populations.
- **Spatial Architecture:** Modeling the Moran process on linear and ring structures to compare how spatial constraints influence evolutionary outcomes.

### 🔍 Historical & Modern Theoretical Concepts
- **Mutation Accumulation:** Analyzing the **Luria-Delbrück fluctuation test** to distinguish between spontaneous and adaptive mutations.
- **Intra-tumour Heterogeneity (ITH):** Using Site-Frequency Spectrum (SFS) analysis to decipher neutral vs. positive selection in cancer genomes.
- **Extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA):** Modeling random vs. non-random segregation of ecDNA and its impact on rapid clonal evolution and treatment resistance.
